Mammals of Brazil

 Mammals are a fascinating class of vertebrates that stand out for unique characteristics, such as presence of hair on the body and mammary glands, which allow the puppies to be fed with milk.

 Mammals are a class of vertebrates that belong to the kingdom Animalia and stand out for their characteristics exclusive features, such as the presence of mammary glands – which produce milk to feed the puppies – and hair, which helps maintain body temperature. In Brazil, it is estimated that there are around 700 species of mammals, covering an enormous diversity, from small rodents to large cats and mammals.

 The diet of mammals is quite varied, reflecting the diversity of species and their habitats. They exist herbivorous mammals, such as capybaras and deer, which feed mainly on plants and vegetation, and you carnivores, such as the jaguar, the largest land predator in the Americas. There are also omnivorous species, as monkeys and giant anteaters, which consume both plants and small animals or insects. They they are present in all Brazilian biomes, such as the Amazon, Cerrado and Pantanal.

General characteristics of mammals

1. Mammary Glands

They produce milk to nourish their young, which is essential for early development.

2. Coat

All mammals have hair at some stage of their lives, helping with thermal regulation and protection.

3. Hot Blood

Mammals are endotherms, maintaining a stable body temperature to survive in varying climates.

4. Developed Nervous System

Larger and more complex brain, providing intelligence and social behavior.

5. Internal Reproduction

Most have embryonic development in the uterus, with a placenta to nourish the embryo.
