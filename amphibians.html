Amphibians of Brazil

 Discover the incredible diversity of amphibians that inhabit Brazil! Dive into the fascinating world of frogs, frogs and tree frogs that live in our biomes and learn more about the country's natural wealth.

 Amphibians, belonging to the class Amphibia, are a group of vertebrates that includes toads, frogs and tree frogs, with more than 1,100 species registered in Brazil, which makes the country one of the richest in diversity of amphibians in the world. Living in diverse environments, these animals are found in biomes such as the Amazon, Atlantic Forest, Cerrado, and even in drier areas of the Caatinga, always in search of humid environments essential for their reproduction and survival.

  The diet of amphibians is mainly based on insects and other small invertebrates, which which makes them important in controlling rules and maintaining ecological balance. They live close to bodies of water, such as rivers, lakes and temporary puddles, where they can lay their eggs and maintain damp skin. Despite their importance, amphibians face threats such as habitat destruction, pollution and climate change, which mainly affect its humid environment.

General characteristics of amphibians

1. Metamorphosis

They go through a process of metamorphosis, moving from the aquatic to the terrestrial environment

2. Permeable and Respiratory Skin

Amphibian skin is thin and permeable, allowing breathing and absorption of water directly from the environment.

3. Poison Glands

Many amphibians have glands in their skin that are poisonous as a defense against predators.

4. Regeneration of Body Parts

Some amphibians have the ability to regenerate limbs and even parts of internal organs.

5. Mimicry and Camouflage

They can change color or camouflage themselves to protect themselves.
