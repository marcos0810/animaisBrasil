Reptiles from Brazil

 Reptiles are a fascinating class of animals that inhabit different environments in Brazil. With a big variety of species, such as snakes, lizards, alligators and turtles.

 Reptiles belong to the Reptilia class, characterized mainly by skin covered with scales. and for They are ectothermic, that is, they depend on the heat of the environment to regulate their body temperature. Node Brazil, there is a great diversity of reptiles, with approximately 800 cataloged species, which inhabit practically all biomes in the country. In the Amazon, for example, we find a rich diversity of snakes and alligators, adapted to the humid climate and dense forests.

 The diet of reptiles is varied and depends on the characteristics of each group. Some snakes, like the Boa constrictors are carnivores and predators, feeding on small mammals, birds and even other reptiles. Lizards, in turn, have more diverse diets: some species are insectivorous, while others They can be omnivorous, consuming plants and small animals. Turtles can be herbivorous, like some freshwater species that live in rivers and lakes, or omnivores, such as sea turtles.

General characteristics of reptiles

1. Scaly Skin

Reptile skin is covered in keratinized scales, which help prevent water loss.

2. Ectothermy (Cold Blood)

They are ectothermic, that is, they depend on the temperature of the environment to regulate their own temperature. body.

3. Longevity

Many reptiles are long-lived, with some species living for several decades.

4. Shedding Skin

During this process, they shed the outer layer of skin to allow for growth.

5. Eggs in Shell

Most reptile species lay eggs with hard or flexible shells.
