Birds of Brazil

 The birds of Brazil represent one of the greatest diversities in the world. From small birds to large birds of prey, they play essential roles in ecosystems, such as in the dispersal of seeds.

 Birds, belonging to the Aves class, represent one of the most diverse groups of vertebrates in the world. Brazil, with approximately 1,900 cataloged species, which represent around 18% of species of known birds in the world. With habitats ranging from dense tropical forests to open areas such as fields and savannas, birds occupy practically all Brazilian biomes, adapting to different environmental and climatic conditions.

  Some birds, such as macaws and toucans, feed mainly on fruits, helping to distribution of seeds in forests such as the Amazon and the Atlantic Forest. Others, like birds of Prey, including hawks and eagles, are carnivorous and hunt small animals. There are also species insectivores. In addition to playing important ecological roles, Brazilian birds also have great cultural and economic value. Many species are icons of regional cultures, present in legends, myths and popular festivals.

General characteristics of birds

1. Pneumatic Bones

Light, hollow bones help with aerodynamics and facilitate flight.

2. Sharp Vision

They can see at great distances and, in some species, in ultraviolet light.

3. Adapted Nozzle

Birds have no teeth and have beaks of various shapes and sizes, adapted to their diet.

4. Flight at Extreme Altitudes

Some birds can fly at impressive altitudes of more than 8,000 meters above sea level

5. Feathers

They serve for protection, thermal insulation and, mainly, for flight.
