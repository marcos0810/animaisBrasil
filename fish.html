Fish from Brazil

 Brazil's fish form an extremely diverse group, with thousands of species found in rivers, lakes and mares that cover the entire national territory.

 Fish, belonging to the Pisces class, are one of the most diverse groups in the animal kingdom, with more of 3,000 species registered in Brazil. These species inhabit a wide variety of environments aquatic, from the rivers and lakes of the Amazon to the oceans along the extensive Brazilian coastline. Some fish are herbivores and feed on algae and aquatic plants, while others are carnivores, hunting small aquatic animals

 In the Amazon region, for example, the Amazon River and its tributaries are rich in species such as tambaqui and pirarucu, while the Pantanal is home to fish adapted to waters with seasonal variations. Already In marine waters and coral reefs, marine fish such as snapper, whiting and marine manatee. Fishing, both in fresh and salt waters, is an important activity, and Many species are traditionally consumed in the North and Northeast regions.

General characteristics of fish

1. Gills

They breathe through gills, which extract oxygen from the water, allowing them to live in aquatic environments

2. Flexible Spine

They have a flexible spine, which allows them to swim efficiently.

3. Fins

They are essential structures for locomotion, stabilization and direction in the aquatic environment

4. Long Migration

Some fish, such as salmon, undertake long migrations, traveling thousands of kilometers

5. Side Lines

They have a sensory system called the lateral line, which detects vibrations and movements in the water
